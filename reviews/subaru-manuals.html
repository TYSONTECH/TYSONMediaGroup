Three Pedals, No Compromise: Subaru’s New Manual Trio

Subaru has handed driving purists a massive reason to celebrate, officially confirming that three brand-new manual-transmission models are slated to arrive. Orchestrated by their newly minted Sports Vehicle Planning Office, this tactical trio skips hybridization entirely, leaning heavily into pure mechanical connection and track-bred engineering honed through the grueling Super Taikyu endurance racing series.

First in line is a hardcore revival of the WRX sedan. Shunning the lighter-duty gearboxes of standard current models, Subaru is reviving the legendary, bulletproof TY85 heavy-duty 6-speed manual straight from the previous-generation WRX STI to handle raw power with maximum durability. Accompanying the sedan is a bespoke, ultra-lightweight BRZ "Complete Car" variant heavily inspired by the track-focused STI Sport Type RA—shedding mass to focus entirely on precision dynamics.

The Return of the Hot Hatch

The most unexpected curveball of the announcement is the development of an all-new 5-door hatchback. Described by Chief Technology Officer Tetsuro Fujinuki as an "affordable base car," this vehicle is being custom-built to let engineers turn raw performance concepts into reality. With distinctive hood scoops pointing to un-electrified, turbocharged boxer power, early indicators suggest this aggressive hatch could lay the perfect groundwork for a spiritual WRX STI flagship successor.

The Manual Trio

Model 1 WRX Sedan (TY85 Heavy-Duty Tech)
Model 2 Lightweight BRZ "Complete Car"
Model 3 Affordable 5-Door Turbo Hatch
Powertrains Non-Electrified Turbo Boxers
